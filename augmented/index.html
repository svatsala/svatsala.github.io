Augmented Reality

By: Shrestha Vatsala & Ethan Shedd

In order to create an augmented reality rendering, we need to map a series of 2D points from the image to 3D points in the real world for every frame of the video. Here, we highlight our methods.

  1. Take a video in the real world of our desired platform → Fig 1
  1. Use Harris corner detector on the first frame to detect corners in the video and save the corners on the box as the interest points
    1. Consequently, save the corresponding 3D points by setting the origin to be the top left of the box and making those points relative to that origin (these are global, not corresponding a particular frame)
  1. Initialize a tracker for the 8x8 patch around each of the 2D points we want to focus on
  1. Update the trackers for each new frame to get the points on the next frame while keeping track of the corresponding 3D points → Fig 2
  1. Use least squares to fit a transformation matrix for each frame that maps each 3D (4D homogenous) point to its corresponding 2D point (3D homogenous)
  1. Finally, use the transformation matrix for each frame to project the axes points defining the AR box onto each frame → Fig 3

Applications & Summary

We learned that augmented reality is essentially a LOT of math. It was also a great way to understand 3d-to-2d transformations and how powerful the least squares algorithm genuinely is. AR itself has many applications in all sorts of modeling - from interior design to medical training - which was quite exciting to realize as well.
